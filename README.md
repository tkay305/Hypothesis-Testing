The question you are trying to determine the answer to is:

> Are more goals scored in women's international soccer matches than men's?

You assume a **10% significance level**, and use the following null and alternative hypotheses:

$H_0$ : The mean number of goals scored in women's international soccer matches is the same as men's.

$H_A$ : The mean number of goals scored in women's international soccer matches is greater than men's.

The distribution of goals scored does not follow a bell-shaped pattern - we cannot assume a normal distribution. We cant make any further assumptions on distribution Wilcoxon-Mann-Whitney test of two groups is the most appropriate as underlying distribution doesn't matter.
As we reject the null, there is sufficient evidence to believe the number of goals scored in women's matches is higher.
